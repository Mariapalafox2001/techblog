Maria Palafox: How old were you when got your first computer?

Silvia Cyaetano: I never had a computer as a kid. My family didn't have the money for luxury items but we had a comfortable life.

Maria Palafox: What was your favorite technology growing up?

Silvia Cayetano: Like I mention before I never had technology growing up, but what we did for fun was do paper airplaines, boats, kites, board games, watch the T.V or do work on the field.

Maria Palafox: Hows has technology changed over your lifetime?

Silvia Cayetano: Technology didn't really affect me since my technology wasn't that advance, also cause it was too expensive to have, but because of this the technology of today is hard for me to understand.

Maria Palafox: Have the changes you've seen in technology over your lifetime mostly help or hurt society?

Silvia Cayetano: I would say that technolgy have helped and hurt society at the same time, cause with out technolgy we wouldn't be able to do what we do today, technology made life easy. But because we have technology, its making society lazy, especially the youth. The youth is more focus on their phones then their enviorment. The youth is being more dependable on technology, that I wonder what would the youth be like if technology was to one day disappear?

Maria Palafox: What is your greatest hope for technology in the future?

Silvia Cayetano: My hope is that technology can somehow help society grow to be active and not lazy.

Maria Palafox: What is your greatest fear for technology in the future?

Silvia Cayetano: My greatest fear for technology would be that technology would one day have a mind of it's own and tries to get rid of us.
